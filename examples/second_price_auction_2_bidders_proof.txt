## Formal Proof of Strategy-Proofness

**Mechanism:**
- Two agents with valuations v₁, v₂ ∈ ℝ≥0.
- Allocation: agent i wins iff bᵢ ≥ bⱼ (ties to agent 1).
- Payment: winner pays the opponent's bid p (= second-highest bid).
- Utility: u_i = (vᵢ − p) if bᵢ ≥ p, else 0.

**Theorem:** For every true valuation v, opponent bid p, and misreport r:
  sp_util(v, v, p) ≥ sp_util(v, r, p)

**Proof by exhaustive case analysis on v vs. p and r vs. p:**

**Case 1: v ≥ p (truth-telling wins)**
  - Truthful utility = v − p ≥ 0.
  - Sub-case 1a: r ≥ p (misreport also wins) → misreport utility = v − p. Equal. ✓
  - Sub-case 1b: r < p (misreport loses)     → misreport utility = 0 ≤ v − p. ✓  (since v ≥ p)

**Case 2: v < p (truth-telling loses)**
  - Truthful utility = 0.
  - Sub-case 2a: r ≥ p (misreport wins)  → misreport utility = v − p < 0 ≤ 0. ✓  (since v < p)
  - Sub-case 2b: r < p (misreport loses) → misreport utility = 0. Equal. ✓

In every case, truth-telling yields weakly higher utility. Since this holds for any opponent bid p and any misreport r, truth-telling is a **dominant strategy** for each agent. □
